On Wed, Aug 06, 2008 at 01:17:29AM +0200, José-Miguel Renieblas wrote:
> I am using jigdo-lite 0.7.2 for Windows in a WinXP Pro SP2 system and I 
> wonder why the downloads stop so often; namely, each 10 to 20 minutes as 
> an average, still sometimes it keeps running about an hour or so.
> 
> Some times pressing "Ctrl-C" resumes the download, but, most of the 
> times after presing that secuence, the message "Do you want to stop the 
> Batch process?" appears. No matter if you press "Y" or "N", the message 
> "Press any key to continue..." (in Spanish) appears and if you do, the 
> program quit.
> 
> I append a screenshot taken when the download freezes.

What happens if you do not interrupt with Ctrl-C? Does the download hang 
indefinitely, or does it display an error after about 15 minutes?

If it's the latter, my guess is that you might trigger some kind of 
bandwidth limit, causing your ISP to make the connection appear to hang to 
prevent you from downloading too much. You cannot really prevent this, but 
you can make the above timeout shorter, e.g. by adding "--timeout=60" to 
the wgetOpts setting in your jigdo-lite-settings.txt file. (Insert it 
before the final ' character!)
